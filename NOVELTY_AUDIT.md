# Novelty Audit

## Established Literature

- BCS theory already provides the microscopic paired state and gap equation.
- Gor'kov already provides the anomalous Green-function formulation.
- Zubarev already provides the double-time Green-function equation of motion.
- Dyson already provides the self-energy/resolvent form.
- BBGKY and Mori-Zwanzig already provide general hierarchy and projection-closure paradigms.
- Hubbard-type EOM treatments already use operator hierarchies for correlated systems.

## Conservative Novelty Claim

The defensible novelty claim is not the gap equation itself. The defensible claim is:

> The reduced BCS Hamiltonian can be reorganized as an explicit Zubarev equation-of-motion hierarchy in which the standard BCS mean-field equations arise as the lowest nontrivial pair-sector closure of the operator hierarchy.

## Demonstrated in the Draft

- Definition of the Liouvillian hierarchy generated from a seed operator.
- Explicit first-level growth from a one-fermion operator into a three-fermion composite operator.
- Algebraic explanation of why the BCS quartic interaction generates higher hierarchy levels.
- Second-order pair-sector return under fermionic anticommutation contractions.
- Dyson-form representation of the projected closure.
- Interpretation of mean-field BCS as a projection rather than as the starting assumption.

## Not Yet Demonstrated

- Exact closure of the full hierarchy.
- Convergence of recursive closure maps.
- Norm estimates for residual hierarchy sectors.
- Any Banach-space, homological, or renormalization-group theorem.
- Monotonicity or universality of the proposed hierarchical information measure.

## Next Scientific Tasks
